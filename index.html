Vulnerable prison population

April 2020

On March 11th the world health organization declared the coronavirus (COVID-19) a world wide pandemic. Coronavirus disease (COVID-19) is an infectious disease caused by a newly discovered coronavirus

“Most people infected with the COVID-19 virus will experience mild to moderate respiratory illness and recover without requiring special treatment. Older people, and those with underlying medical problems like cardiovascular disease, diabetes, chronic respiratory disease, and cancer are more likely to develop serious illness.” - World Health Organization.

With this the growing concern of how to mitigate the damage from COVID-19 I decided to do some research which because of the rapid changing news led me into a lot of different directions. One direction in particular caught my attention, and that was the vulnerability of the prison population. Coronavirus effects on human rights podcast March 19th, episode) In this podcast they talked about how there may be a problem when it come to protecting people in prisons from catching and transmitting the virus, because they are unable to take the same precautions that the rest of the population can i.e. social distancing.

The United States has the largest prison population in the world. Around the world cities, and even countries have wrestled with what to do to prevent this particular population from being so negatively affected. Besides the United States there are three countries in particular that have had different approaches and consequences when it comes to inmates and protection from COVID-19 these include Brazil, Italy, and Iran.




Brazil:

Brazil is a country that also has a very large prison population. On March 16th hundreds of prisons were not allowed to leave the semi-open facility for easter prison holidays were canceled and visitor restrictions were tightened.

Italy:

Similar to Brazil Italy has seen riots and escapes of prisoners due to fear of coronavirus. The Italian government adopted a decree to allow prisons with less than 18 months on their sentences to have early release. This unfortunately was not enough because in early march 50 prisons in italy experienced riots due to anxiety and restrictions placed by prison authorities. In addition to these restrictions there have been measures taken to provide prisons and guards with mask, gloves, and temperature checks.

United States:

As of late March the United States has started to release prisoners from cities around the country following in the path of Iran. Prsion response in the USA
